Daily Current Affairs 17 November 2025:-

1. 1. National Press Day was observed across India on November 16, marking the establishment of the Press Council of India in 1966.
2. 2. The 56th International Film Festival of India (IFFI) 2025 is all set to commence in Goa.
3. 3. The National Investigation Agency (NIA) arrested a Kashmiri man in connection with the car explosion near the Red Fort metro station in Delhi.
4. 4. Minister of State for Defence, Sanjay Seth, is leading an Indian delegation to the Dubai Air Show 2025 (Nov 17-18) in the UAE, where the Indian Air Force is showcasing the Suryakiran Aerobatic Team and LCA Tejas.
5. 5. India became the world's 6th largest Patent Filer in the world, with over 64,000 patents filed, as per the latest reports.
6. 6. The government launched the “Digital Rural India Mission 2025” to boost internet and e-learning facilities in villages.
7. 7. The Ministry of Electronics and IT (MeitY) notified the long-awaited data protection rules, paving the way for India to have a functional privacy law.
8. 8. The Central Government has approved the rationalisation of royalty rates for four critical minerals: graphite, caesium, rubidium, and zirconium.
9. 9. Railways rolled out an AI-based fraud detection system to curb ticketing scams across its network.
10. 10. Schools in parts of Tamil Nadu, including Chennai, Tiruvallur, and Chengalpattu, remained closed due to heavy rain and flooding.
11. 11. J&K has been recognized as a top achiever in ease of doing business at the Udyog Samagam 2025.
12. 12. The National Democratic Alliance (NDA) began final talks to form the new government in Bihar following its victory in the Assembly elections, with the swearing-in ceremony expected around November 20.
13. 13. The Malabar Naval Exercise 2025 between the navies of India, the US, Australia, and Japan kicked off on November 12 in the Northern Pacific.
14. 14. At the COP30 summit in Belém, Brazil, climate activists urged governments to phase out fossil fuels.
15. 15. National Epilepsy Day is observed annually in India on November 17 to raise public awareness about the neurological disorder.
