Daily Current Affairs 19 November 2025:-

1. 1. International Men's Day is observed annually on November 19 to celebrate the positive value men bring to the world, their families, and communities, with the 2025 theme being “Celebrating Men and Boys.”
2. 2. World Toilet Day is also observed on November 19 to raise awareness of the global sanitation crisis and is linked to Sustainable Development Goal 6 (water and sanitation for all).
3. 3. The 8th edition of the joint bilateral military exercise 'Ajeya Warrior' between the Indian Army and the British Army commenced at the Mahajan Field Firing Ranges (MFFR) in Bikaner, Rajasthan.
4. 4. The Geographical Indications Registry (GIR) granted the GI tag to Ambaji White Marble from Gujarat's prominent pilgrimage site and Shaktipeeth.
5. 5. The Union Cabinet approved 17 new projects with a total investment of around ₹7,172 crore under the 2nd tranche of the Electronic Components and Semiconductor Manufacturing (ECMS) scheme.
6. 6. The Supreme Court (SC) recalled the ban on ex-post facto Environmental Clearances (EC), emphasizing that granting retrospective environmental clearance violates the precautionary principle.
7. 7. The Union Ministry of Food and Public Distribution launched the ASHA (Anna Sahayata Holistic AI Solution) AI-based platform to allow Public Distribution System (PDS) beneficiaries to share feedback on ration distribution.
8. 8. The 16th Finance Commission (FC), chaired by Dr. Aravind Panagariya, is set to submit its report to the President, covering the period 2026-27 to 2030-31.
9. 9. The Department of Consumer Affairs (DoCA)'s Unified Digital Platform, e-Jagriti (for consumer grievance redressal), attracted over 2.75 lakh registered users.
10. 10. The Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and Gati Shakti Vishwavidyalya (GSV) signed an MoU to develop smart technology solutions for national security, focusing on logistics R&D.
11. 11. The Global Methane Status Report 2025, released by UNEP, noted that while projected emissions growth has slowed, current commitments will fail to meet the 2030 target.
12. 12. India was Re-Elected to the Codex Executive Committee at the 48th session of the Codex Alimentarius Commission (CAC48), reaffirming its leadership in global food standards.
13. 13. The Union Minister for Electronics and IT launched the YUVA AI for ALL Initiative under the IndiaAI Mission, aiming to empower 1 crore (10 million) Indians with basic AI knowledge.
14. 14. India is set to host the Global Big Cats Summit in New Delhi in 2026 to foster international cooperation on conserving big cat species.
15. 15. Brazil designated 10 new Indigenous Territories through a presidential decree, granting them legal protection for culture, land rights, forests, and biodiversity.
