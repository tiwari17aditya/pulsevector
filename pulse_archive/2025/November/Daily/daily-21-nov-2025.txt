Daily Current Affairs 21 November 2025:-

1. 1. World Fisheries Day 2025 is observed annually on November 21 to highlight the importance of healthy ocean ecosystems and sustainable fisheries; the 2025 theme is India's Blue Transformation: Strengthening Value Addition in Seafood Exports.
2. 2. HDFC Bank emerged as India's Most Valuable Brand in Kantar's Brandz Top 100 Most Valuable Indian Brands 2025 report, surpassing Tata Consultancy Services (TCS).
3. 3. The blueprint for India's First Quantum City (Q-City), proposed at Hesaraghatta, Bengaluru (Karnataka), was revealed at the 28th Bengaluru Tech Summit (BTS 2025).
4. 4. The 2nd edition of the Bharatiya Kalamahotsav will be inaugurated by President Draupadi Murmu.
5. 5. NITI Aayog's Atal Innovation Mission (AIM) partnered with CFA Institute India Private Limited to boost financial literacy and ethical decision-making in India's innovation ecosystem.
6. 6. The Supreme Court of India delivered an advisory opinion stating there are no fixed timelines for Governors to act on Bills passed by the State Legislature.
7. 7. The Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH) launched “Project Gaja-Lok: Elephant Lands and their Cultural Symbolism in Asia” to document the cultural and ecological relationship between Asian elephants and humans.
8. 8. India's defence production achieved its highest-ever value of ₹1.54 Lakh Crore in the financial year 2024-25.
9. 9. World Television Day 2025 is observed annually on November 21 to commemorate the date the first World Television Forum was held in 1996.
10. 10. The India Justice Report (IJR) revealed that over 55% of cases before India's Juvenile Justice Boards (JJBs) were pending as of October 31, 2023, highlighting systemic gaps.
11. 11. The ICC unveiled the match schedule for the ICC U19 Men's Cricket World Cup 2026.
12. 12. Sarayu Velpula from Telangana became India's 26th Woman Grandmaster (WGM) in chess.
13. 13. Mexico's Fátima Bosch was crowned Miss Universe 2025.
14. 14. The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change announced that the National Board for Wildlife (NBA) released ₹39.84 crore to Andhra Pradesh for Red Sanders Protection and Conservation.
15. 15. Nitish Kumar was sworn in again as the Chief Minister of Bihar, along with 26 other ministers.
