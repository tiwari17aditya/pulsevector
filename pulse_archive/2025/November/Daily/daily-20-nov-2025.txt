Daily Current Affairs 20 November 2025:-

1. 1. Nitish Kumar takes oath as the Chief Minister of Bihar for a record 10th time, marking an unprecedented return and signalling continuity in the state's political leadership.
2. 2. The Supreme Court pronounced its advisory opinion on a Presidential Reference, ruling that courts cannot fix a timeline for the President or Governors to act on bills passed by the assembly.
3. 3. The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) launched 'YUVA AI for ALL', a free national Artificial Intelligence (AI) course under the IndiaAI Mission, aiming to empower one crore citizens with foundational AI skills.
4. 4. India has been unanimously re-elected to the Executive Committee of the Codex Alimentarius Commission (CCEXEC) for the Asia Region during the 48th Session of the CAC.
5. 5. The Sixth National Water Awards 2024 were presented, with Maharashtra winning the Best State award, followed by Gujarat. Navi Mumbai, Maharashtra, won Best Urban Local Body.
6. 6. The Supreme Court ordered the Jharkhand government to declare 31,468 hectares of Saranda forest as a wildlife sanctuary within three months.
7. 7. The Ministry of Health launched the National Action Plan on Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR 2.0) (2025–29), bringing together 20 ministries to curb antibiotic resistance.
8. 8. The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change approved a Leopard Birth-Control Programme in Maharashtra to address the rising human-leopard conflict in the state.
9. 9. Keerthy Suresh was appointed as UNICEF India's new Celebrity Advocate to promote and protect child rights across the country.
10. 10. The Government of Andhra Pradesh signed an agreement with the World Economic Forum (WEF) to establish a dedicated Centre for Energy and Cyber Resilience (CECR) in Visakhapatnam.
11. 11. India's trade deficit widened to a record USD 41.68 billion in October 2025, primarily due to a rise in gold imports and a slip in goods exports.
12. 12. Infibeam Avenues received a Payment Aggregator (PA) Perpetual Licence from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), making it the fourth such licence for the company.
13. 13. India announced at COP30 in Belém, Brazil, that it will host the Global Big Cats Summit in New Delhi in 2026 under the International Big Cat Alliance (IBCA) framework.
14. 14. The Kodaikanal Solar Observatory (KoSO) digitised its century-long solar observations, creating the first reliable, century-long reconstruction of solar magnetism publicly available.
15. 15. Iran has suspended visa-free entry for Indian citizens, stating the facility meant for tourism was being misused for job-seeking purposes.
