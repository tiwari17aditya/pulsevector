Daily Current Affairs 10 Nov 2025:-

1. 1. World Science Day for Peace and Development was observed on November 10, with the theme "Unleash Your Inner Scientist."
2. 2. HAL signed a $1 Billion deal with GE for the supply of 113 F404 engines to power the 97 LCA Mk 1A fighters.
3. 3. The Dumpsite Remediation Accelerator Programme (DRAP) and the Urban Investment Window (UiWIN) were launched at the National Urban Conclave 2025 to clear legacy dumpsites by 2026.
4. 4. Mumbai was ranked as Asia's happiest city in 2025 by a Time Out survey of 18,000+ residents.
5. 5. India joined the Tropical Forests Forever Facility (TFFF), a Brazil-led global fund to reward tropical countries for forest protection, as an observer.
6. 6. The RBI repatriated 274 tonnes of India's gold between March 2023 and September 2025, raising domestic holdings to 575.8 tonnes to strengthen reserves.
7. 7. INS Sahyadri participated in key Quad Naval Drills in Guam to boost interoperability and maritime security in the Indo-Pacific.
8. 8. The 18th Urban Mobility India (UMI) Conference & Exhibition 2025 was inaugurated in Gurugram by the Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs and Power.
9. 9. The Assam Cabinet approved the Assam Prohibition of Polygamy Bill, 2025, which seeks to criminalize polygamy across the state, with certain exceptions.
10. 10. President Droupadi Murmu is on a State Visit to Angola, marking the first-ever visit by an Indian President to the southern African nation.
11. 11. India and Ecuador signed an MoU to boost cooperation between their diplomatic training institutions, and the resident Embassy of India in Quito was jointly inaugurated.
12. 12. Wholesale inflation (WPI) in India slipped to -1.21% in October 2025, driven by falling food, fuel, and metal prices.
13. 13. India's first Tribal-Focused Genome Project was launched as part of the Genome India Project to sequence 2,000 tribal genomes across 17 districts.
14. 14. Veteran Archaeologist Ravindra Korisettar from Dharwad was honoured with the Rajyotsava Award.
15. 15. ReNew Energy secured a $477 million ADB funding for India's first round-the-clock peak renewable energy project with battery storage in Andhra Pradesh.
