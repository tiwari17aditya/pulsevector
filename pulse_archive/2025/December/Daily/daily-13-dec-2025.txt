Daily Current Affairs 13 December 2025:-

1. 1. Nobel Peace Prize :- World Food Programme (WFP) launched a 'Digital Food Assistance' system in Nigeria, providing cashless aid.
2. 2. India-Russia Summit :- The 23rd annual summit emphasized defense cooperation, space technology, and energy partnership between the nations.
3. 3. COP28 Climate Talks :- Delegates agreed on a phase-down of fossil fuels and established a loss and damage fund for vulnerable countries.
4. 4. ISRO's New Satellite :- ISRO successfully launched the advanced communication satellite GSAT-20 from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre.
5. 5. Global Refugee Crisis :- UNHCR reported a record 120 million forcibly displaced people worldwide due to conflict and persecution.
6. 6. Digital India Act :- The new legislation was introduced in Parliament to replace the outdated Information Technology Act of 2000.
7. 7. Railway Modernization :- Indian Railways initiated a project to introduce Vande Bharat sleeper trains on all major long-distance routes.
8. 8. RBI's Monetary Policy :- The Reserve Bank of India maintained the repo rate at 6.5% citing concerns over persistent food inflation.
9. 9. Sports Achievement :- Indian Badminton player Lakshya Sen won the prestigious BWF World Tour Finals title in China.
10. 10. Aviation Sector Growth :- India became the third-largest domestic aviation market globally, seeing a 15% increase in passenger traffic.
11. 11. Health Sector Initiative :- Government launched the 'Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission' app to link health records digitally.
12. 12. Archaeological Discovery :- A 2,500-year-old Buddhist stupa was unearthed by archaeologists in the coastal state of Andhra Pradesh.
13. 13. International Film Festival :- The 56th International Film Festival of India (IFFI) concluded in Goa with the Golden Peacock award.
14. 14. New Defence Acquisition :- Ministry of Defence approved the purchase of advanced fighter aircraft from a European consortium.
15. 15. Agricultural Scheme :- A new scheme was introduced to promote the cultivation of millets and boost farmer incomes in arid regions.
